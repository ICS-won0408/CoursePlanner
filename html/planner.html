The Course Planner is an online tool used for planning courses at ICS. It provides information and student ratings for each course.

Requirements

In order to graduate, you must meet the minimum credit requirement, which is 25 credits. In addition to that, you must also have the minimum credit requirement for each type of course:

  • Science: 3.0 credits
  • Math: 3.0 credits
  • Fine & Performing Arts: 3.0 credits
  • Social Science: 3.0 credits
  • Language Arts: 4.0 credits
  • World Languages: 2.0 credits
  • Physical Education: 2.0 credits
  • Bible: 1.0 credit per year at ICS
  • Information and Technology Literacy: 0.5 credits
  • Health: 0.5 credits

It is important to check college application requirements when considering which courses to take. Some collegees may want to see more of a certain type of course. College entry requirements vary from country to country. Taking the absolute minimum amount of courses is definitely not recommended and will be detrimental to your college applications. Taking to many courses will be extremely stressful and so you should carefully consider which courses you want to take.

If you moved to ICS during high school, it is important to make sure your credits from your previous school have been properly transferred over.

Grade 9 and 10 students must take at least 8 credits during the school year. Grade 11 and 12 students must take 7 credits. Study halls are assigned to blocks in which you do not have a class, with the exception of seniors. Study halls are for cleaning up your homework load or for studying for a quiz or test.

Required Courses

Throughout your high school, there will be courses that you are required to take in order to graduate. You should factor in these courses when managing your schedule. Any failed mandatory course must be retaken

  • Grade 9: Language Arts, Modern European History, New Testament - Acts & Epistles, (Honors)Biology, PE
  • Grade 10: World Religions, Biblical Ethics, Asian Studies, PE
  • Grade 11: Life Calling
  • Grade 12: Christian Apologetics
  • Health must be taken in either 10th grade or above.
